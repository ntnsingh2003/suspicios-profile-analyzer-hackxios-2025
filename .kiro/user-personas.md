# User Personas: Suspicious Profile Analyzer

## Persona 1: Sarah Chen - Regular Social Media User

**Demographics**: 28, Marketing Manager, Active on Instagram, Facebook, and dating apps
**Tech Comfort**: Moderate - uses apps daily but not security-focused

### Goals
- Connect with genuine people for dating and networking
- Maintain active social media presence without security risks
- Avoid falling victim to scams while remaining open to new connections
- Learn to identify red flags for future protection

### Pain Points
- **Overwhelmed by Suspicious Messages**: Receives multiple friend requests and DMs from attractive strangers but can't distinguish legitimate from malicious
- **Fear of Missing Real Connections**: Worried about ignoring genuine people while trying to avoid scammers
- **Lack of Clear Warning Signs**: Doesn't know what specific red flags to look for beyond "too good to be true"
- **Time-Consuming Manual Research**: Spends excessive time reverse-searching images and investigating profiles
- **Emotional Manipulation Vulnerability**: Struggles to recognize gradual trust-building tactics used by sophisticated scammers

### Expectations from System
- **Simple Risk Indicators**: Clear, color-coded risk levels (green/yellow/red) that she can understand instantly
- **Plain English Explanations**: "This profile shows signs of using stolen photos" rather than technical jargon
- **Actionable Safety Recommendations**: Specific advice like "Ask for a video call" or "Don't share personal information"
- **Non-Intrusive Warnings**: Alerts that don't interrupt her social experience but keep her protected
- **Mobile-Friendly Interface**: Works seamlessly within her existing apps and social media workflows

## Persona 2: Marcus Rodriguez - Platform Trust & Safety Manager

**Demographics**: 35, Senior Trust & Safety Analyst at major social platform, 8 years experience in content moderation
**Tech Comfort**: High - works daily with moderation tools, data analytics, and security systems

### Goals
- Protect platform users from malicious actors and fraudulent content
- Maintain user trust while minimizing false positive account suspensions
- Demonstrate regulatory compliance and due diligence to executives and auditors
- Scale threat detection to match platform growth and evolving attack methods

### Pain Points
- **Scale vs Accuracy Trade-off**: Current tools either miss sophisticated threats or flag too many legitimate users
- **Lack of Explainable Decisions**: Cannot explain to users, executives, or regulators why specific accounts were flagged or suspended
- **Manual Review Bottleneck**: Team overwhelmed reviewing flagged accounts, needs better automated prioritization
- **Rapidly Evolving Threats**: Scammers adapt faster than rule-based systems can be updated to counter new tactics
- **Regulatory Compliance Pressure**: Must demonstrate proactive user protection while avoiding over-censorship accusations

### Expectations from System
- **High-Confidence Risk Scores**: Precise risk assessments backed by specific, auditable evidence for regulatory compliance
- **Bulk Analysis Capabilities**: Process thousands of profiles efficiently during coordinated threat investigations
- **API Integration**: Seamless integration with existing moderation workflows and case management systems
- **Customizable Detection Thresholds**: Ability to adjust sensitivity based on platform type, user demographics, and threat landscape
- **Comprehensive Audit Trails**: Detailed logs and analytics for trend analysis, executive reporting, and regulatory documentation

## Persona 3: David Kim - Job Seeker on Hiring Platform

**Demographics**: 24, Recent college graduate, Actively job hunting on LinkedIn, Indeed, AngelList
**Tech Comfort**: High - Digital native, comfortable with technology but inexperienced with professional networking

### Goals
- Find legitimate employment opportunities quickly in competitive job market
- Build professional network without falling victim to job fraud schemes
- Learn to distinguish between real recruiters and malicious actors
- Protect personal information while remaining accessible to potential employers

### Pain Points
- **Urgent Job Search Pressure**: Desperation for employment makes him vulnerable to "too good to be true" job offers
- **Recruiter Legitimacy Uncertainty**: Cannot distinguish between genuine HR professionals and sophisticated job fraud scammers
- **Personal Information Exposure Risk**: Unsure when it's appropriate to share SSN, references, or banking details during hiring process
- **Professional Network Inexperience**: Doesn't understand normal recruiting practices versus red flag behaviors
- **Fear of Missing Opportunities**: Worried about being overly cautious and losing genuine job chances to other candidates

### Expectations from System
- **Recruiter Profile Verification**: Clear indicators showing whether a recruiter profile is legitimate or potentially fraudulent
- **Job Posting Risk Assessment**: Automated warnings about posts requesting upfront payments or unusual personal information
- **Educational Security Guidance**: Explanations that help him learn to identify future threats independently
- **Professional Context Awareness**: Risk assessments tailored specifically to job hunting scenarios and recruiting industry norms
- **Privacy-Preserving Analysis**: Assurance that his own profile analysis won't be shared with potential employers or affect his job prospects
